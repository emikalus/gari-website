Our mission

Build practical accessibility tools that people can actually use.

GARI exists to research, prototype, and deploy assistive technology solutions for real people facing real challenges.

Why this matters

When a child is newly diagnosed with diabetes, parents suddenly have to calculate insulin doses, track blood sugar, count carbohydrates, manage supplies, and make critical decisions multiple times a day.

  • ✓ Reduce stress during the transition home
  • ✓ Improve organization around daily care
  • ✓ Help families keep learning after hospital education
  • ✓ Support routines without replacing clinical guidance
The first prototype

Insulin Buddy combines education, organization, and daily support in one station.

The goal is simple: reduce stress, improve organization, help families learn, and make daily diabetes management feel less overwhelming.

🧮

Guided calculations

Organizes the steps families learn for meal-time and correction-dose decisions.

🗂️

Questions donors may ask

Clear answers build trust.

Is Insulin Buddy a medical device?

Insulin Buddy is intended as an educational and organizational support tool. It does not replace clinical guidance, diabetes education, or instructions from a licensed healthcare professional.

What will donations pay for?

Donations support prototype hardware, software development, 3D printing, education content, testing materials, and sponsored units for families.

Who is the first target user?

The first target users are families with children who are newly diagnosed with diabetes and are learning to manage care at home.

How can hospitals or educators help?

Educators, nurses, clinicians, and community partners can review prototypes, suggest improvements, and help identify families who may benefit from sponsored units.
